01 Visual Design Visual hierarchy, consistency & accessible design
--
Section Score
0 of 5 checked
Critical items required
✓ Sub-Category Checklist Item Owner Int
Layout & Hierarchy Layout follows a top-down, left-right reading order based on importance. Titles, KPI blocks, charts, and tables follow a clear, consistent hierarchy across the dashboard. DS Critical
Why it mattersUsers grasp the main story and priority insights without needing a walkthrough.
Consistency & Standards Apply approved colors, fonts, and sizing rules from the visual guide. Use consistent spacing and alignment throughout. Use the same color to represent the same concept within the dashboard. DS Rec
Why it mattersDashboards look and feel like a cohesive MIDAS product and are easy to scan.
Accessibility Colors meet contrast guidelines for readability. Color choices pass a color blind simulator test. Font sizes meet minimum readability standards. DS Critical
Why it mattersDashboards are usable by stakeholders with visual limitations and meet baseline accessibility expectations.
Chart Selection & Clarity Chart types match the analytical purpose: comparison, trend, distribution, or composition. Axes, labels, and legends are clear and accurate. Complex or custom charts are avoided unless aligned with the lead stakeholder. DS MA Critical
Why it mattersCharts match the analytical question and make patterns easy to understand at a glance.
UI & Interaction Elements Buttons, menus, tooltips, legends, and icons follow design specifications. Interactive elements have consistent hover and active states. Non-interactive text does not appear clickable. DS Rec
Why it mattersInteractive elements feel intentional and stakeholders can clearly distinguish what is clickable.
02 Usability Fast, clear & insightful for the intended stakeholder
--
Section Score
0 of 7 checked
Critical items required
✓ Sub-Category Checklist Item Owner Int
Clarity & Guidance All language is in plain English and tailored to the intended stakeholder. User-facing fields use stakeholder-friendly names. A "Start Here" or "How to Use" section is included where the dashboard requires it. DS MA Critical
Why it mattersStakeholders can understand labels and navigate the dashboard without a walkthrough.
Performance Dashboard initial load is under 10 seconds. Common interactions respond within 5 to 10 seconds. Extracts and context filters are used where they improve load speed. DS Critical
Why it mattersPerformance never blocks usage or decision-making.
Devices & Layout Dashboard is designed for 1300 × 800 pixels (16:9). No horizontal scrolling required. Containers and tiled layouts are used to keep the layout stable across screen sizes. DS Critical
Why it mattersThe dashboard renders predictably on standard laptop screens without layout breakage.
Narrative & Exploration Annotations explain what changed or why it matters. Drill-throughs or detail tabs are available where relevant. Actions and highlights make selections visible and intentional. DS MA Rec
Why it mattersNarrative and interactions deepen understanding without adding visual clutter.
Filter Clarity & Consistency Filters are ordered and grouped logically, left to right and top to bottom. Primary filters are visible on load. Filters apply consistently across views, or exceptions are clearly labeled. DS Critical
Why it mattersFilters behave predictably and reduce confusion for all stakeholders.
Filter Efficiency & UX Apply buttons are enabled on all multi-select filters. Data loads only after the user finishes making selections. DS Critical
Why it mattersUsers are not waiting on every individual selection. Filters are efficient and do not overwhelm.
Exports Export views align to dashboard visuals. Sheets are clearly named. Export table headers match dashboard field labels. Minimal manual cleanup is required after export. DS Rec
Why it mattersUsers can export data that is immediately usable without reformatting.
